Data Protection

Ensuring secure handling of company and customer data

Data Classification

Understanding different levels of data sensitivity

All data must be classified according to its sensitivity level to ensure appropriate protection measures are applied.

Public

Public Information

Information that can be freely shared with the public without risk.

Examples:

  • Published marketing materials
  • Public website contents
  • Press releases and announcements
  • General company information
Protection: No special handling required. Can be stored anywhere.
Internal

Internal Information

Information intended for use within the organization only.

Examples:

  • Internal policies and procedures
  • Employee directories
  • Project documentation (non-sensitive)
  • Internal communications and memos
Protection: Store on company systems only. Limit sharing to employees. Use password protection for email attachments.
Confidential

Confidential Information

Highly sensitive information requiring the strictest protection.

Examples:

  • Customer personal data (PII)
  • Financial records and contracts
  • Proprietary client information
  • Security credentials and keys
  • Strategic business plans
Protection: Encryption required. Strict access controls. MFA mandatory. Never share outside authorized personnel. Secure deletion when no longer needed.

Data Handling Procedures

Guidelines for accessing and using sensitive data

General Data Handling Rules

Do

  • Only access data necessary for your work
  • Encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Use secure file transfer methods
  • Lock your screen when handling sensitive data
  • Classify data before sharing or storing

Don't

  • Share data with unauthorized individuals
  • Store sensitive data on personal devices
  • Use unencrypted email for confidential data
  • Leave printed confidential documents unattended
  • Take customer data outside approved systems

Customer Data Protection

  1. Compliance First: Always follow customer-specific data protection requirements and contracts
  2. Minimize Collection: Only collect and retain customer data that is absolutely necessary
  3. Secure Processing: Use customer-approved tools and environments for processing their data
  4. Incident Reporting: Report any customer data incidents to management immediately
  5. Proper Disposal: Securely delete customer data when the project concludes

Personal Data (PII) Guidelines

Personal Identifiable Information (PII) requires extra care and compliance with data protection regulations:

What is PII?

  • Names, addresses
  • Email, phone numbers
  • ID numbers
  • Financial information
  • Health records

Protection Required

  • Encryption at all times
  • Access logging
  • Regular audits
  • Data minimization
  • Consent tracking

Your Responsibility

  • Process lawfully
  • Limit access
  • Report breaches
  • Respect privacy rights
  • Secure deletion

Storage & Retention

Where to store data and when to delete it

Approved Storage Locations

Company-Managed Systems

  • Company file servers and SharePoint
  • Approved cloud storage
  • Customer-provided secure environments
  • IT-approved project management tools
Prohibited Storage Locations: Personal cloud accounts, personal email accounts, unencrypted USB drives, personal devices without company management, and unapproved third-party services.

Data Retention Periods

Active Project Data
Data related to ongoing projects Project Duration + 1 year
Customer Data
Per customer contract requirements Contract Specified
Financial Records
Invoices, receipts, tax documents 7 years
Employee Records
Personnel files and evaluations 5 years post-employment
Email and Communications
General business correspondence 2 years

Secure Data Deletion

  • Digital Data: Use secure deletion tools that overwrite data
  • Physical Documents: Use cross-cut shredders for confidential papers
  • Storage Devices: Contact IT for proper disposal of hard drives and USB devices
  • Cloud Data: Ensure data is removed from all backups and archives
  • Customer Data: Follow customer-specific deletion requirements and obtain confirmation
A Legal Hold Exception: Do not delete any data subject to legal hold, litigation, or ongoing investigation.
