What is a Security Incident?

Understanding what qualifies as a security incident

A security incident is any event that could compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company or customer data, systems, or networks.

Common Examples of Security Incidents

Data Breaches

  • Unauthorized access to systems
  • Lost or stolen devices with data
  • Accidental data exposure
  • Data sent to wrong recipients

Malware & Attacks

  • Virus or ransomware infection
  • Phishing attack attempts
  • Suspicious network activity
  • Unauthorized software installation

Account Compromises

  • Stolen or shared credentials
  • Unauthorized account access
  • Suspicious login attempts
  • Account takeover

Policy Violations

  • Unauthorized data transfers
  • Misuse of systems or access
  • Bypassing security controls
  • Failure to follow procedures
Remember:
When in doubt, report it. It is always better to report a potential incident that turns out to be nothing than to ignore a real security threat.

Immediate Response Actions

What to do when you discover a security incident

Follow these steps immediately when you identify or suspect a security incident:

1
Stay Calm & Don't Panic

Take a deep breath. Security incidents happen, and we have processes to handle them.

2
Contain the Incident (If Safe)
  • Disconnect from the network if needed
  • Lock your computer to prevent unauthorized access
  • Change compromised passwords immediately
  • Do not delete files or logs
3
Document What Happened
  • What did you observe?
  • When did it happen?
  • What systems or data were affected?
  • What actions did you take?
  • Any usernames, IP addresses, or identifiers
4
Report Immediately

Contact IT Security as soon as possible. Time is critical. The faster we know about an incident, the faster we can respond and minimize damage.

5
Follow Instructions

Work with the IT Security team. They may ask you to preserve evidence, provide more information, or take specific actions.

Important Don'ts

  • Don't try to investigate alone
  • Don't delete anything
  • Don't share incident details publicly
  • Don't wait to report

When to Use Each Method

High Severity: Active attacks, ransomware, large data breaches, system compromises, stolen devices with sensitive data
Medium Severity: Suspicious phishing, policy violations, minor data exposures, suspicious activity
Low Severity: Security questions, policy clarification, potential vulnerabilities, improvement suggestions

Confidential Reporting & Whistleblowing

Report concerns safely and anonymously

Safe Reporting Culture

We are committed to maintaining a safe environment where employees can report security concerns, policy violations, or unethical behavior without fear of retaliation.

  • No Retaliation: You will not face negative consequences for reporting concerns in good faith
  • Confidentiality: Your identity will be protected
  • Anonymity Option: You can report anonymously if you prefer

What You Can Report Confidentially

  • Security policy violations
  • Unauthorized system access
  • Data handling violations
  • Inadequate security controls
  • Cover-ups of incidents

Ethical Concerns

  • Fraud or financial misconduct
  • Harassment or discrimination
  • Conflicts of interest
  • Misuse of company resources
  • Violation of laws or regulations

What Happens After You Report

  1. Your report is received and acknowledged

  5. Appropriate action is taken based on findings
  6. You receive an outcome update when possible
